# Dashboard Overview

The Dashboard is the first thing you see when you open FXFlow. Think of it like the home screen on your phone — it shows you all the important stuff in one place so you don't have to go hunting for it.

## Hero Metrics

At the very top of the Dashboard, you'll see a row of big numbers. These are your **hero metrics** — the most important numbers about your trading account.

- **Balance** — This is the total amount of money in your account, not counting any trades that are still open. Think of it like the amount sitting in your piggy bank right now.
- **Equity** — This is your balance _plus_ whatever your open trades are currently worth. If your open trades are making money, equity is higher than balance. If they're losing, it's lower.
- **Open P/L** — Short for "Open Profit/Loss." This shows how much money your currently-open trades are making or losing _right now_. It changes constantly because prices move all the time.
- **Margin Used** — When you open a trade, you have to set aside some money as a deposit (called "margin"). This number shows how much of your money is tied up as deposits.
- **Day P/L** — How much you've made or lost just today. Resets every day so you can track daily performance.

> [!TIP]
> Green numbers mean profit. Red numbers mean loss. If everything is green, you're having a good day!

## Trade Spotlight

Below the hero metrics, you'll find the **Trade Spotlight**. This section shows your current live trades — the ones that are open right now and actively making or losing money.

Each trade shows the currency pair (like EUR/USD), whether you're buying or selling, and how much profit or loss it's at. It's like a scoreboard for your active trades.

## Activity Feed

The **Activity Feed** is a timeline of recent events — trades being opened, closed, modified, or signals coming in. Think of it like a social media feed, but instead of posts from friends, it shows things happening in your trading account.

The newest events appear at the top, so you always see what just happened first.

## Automation Status Bar

FXFlow can trade automatically for you using different systems. The **Automation Status Bar** shows which of these systems are currently running:

- **Trade Finder** — A scanner that looks for trading opportunities based on your rules
- **TV Alerts** — Receives signals from TradingView (another trading tool) and acts on them
- **EdgeFinder** — An artificial intelligence system that finds and places trades for you

Each one shows a small colored dot: green means it's running, gray means it's turned off.

> [!NOTE]
> You don't have to use any automation features. Many traders prefer to place every trade by hand. These are completely optional.

## Source Performance

This section breaks down your profits and losses by _how_ each trade was created. For example, you can see whether your manual trades are doing better than your automated ones.

Sources include things like "FXFlow" (trades you placed yourself), "TradingView Alert" (automated signals), "Trade Finder" (the scanner), and "EdgeFinder" (placed by the AI system).

This helps you figure out which methods are actually making you money.

## Market Calendar

The **Market Calendar** shows upcoming economic events that can affect currency prices. Things like government reports on jobs, inflation, or interest rate decisions.

> [!FOREX]
> Currency prices can move a LOT when big economic reports come out. Imagine the government announces that a country's economy is doing really well — that country's currency might suddenly jump in value. The calendar warns you so you're not caught off guard.

## AI Insights

If you use any of FXFlow's AI features (like AI Analysis or EdgeFinder), this card shows how much you've used them. AI features cost a small amount per use (called "tokens"), and this section tracks:

- How many tokens you've used
- The estimated cost
- A breakdown by which AI feature used them

> [!TIP]
> Think of AI tokens like credits at an arcade. Each time the AI does something for you, it uses some credits. This card helps you keep track of your spending.
