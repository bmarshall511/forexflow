# Placing Orders

Placing an order is how you start a trade. You're telling FXFlow "I want to buy (or sell) this currency pair, at this price, with these safety levels." This guide walks you through every part of the Order Ticket — the form you fill out to create a new trade.

## The Order Ticket

The Order Ticket is a form that appears when you want to place a new trade. It has several fields to fill in. Don't worry — we'll explain each one.

## Order Types

The first choice you make is what _type_ of order you want:

### Market Order

A market order executes **immediately** at whatever the current price is. You're saying "I want in right now, at today's price."

> [!FOREX]
> A market order is like walking into a store and buying something at the price on the tag. You pay what it costs right now — no waiting.

### Limit Order

A limit order **waits** until the price reaches a level you choose. You set the price you want, and the order sits there until the market comes to you.

> [!FOREX]
> A limit order is like telling a store "I'll buy this jacket, but only if it goes on sale to $30." The order waits in the background, and if the price drops to $30, it automatically buys for you. If the price never reaches $30, nothing happens.

## Buy vs. Sell

Every trade has a direction:

- **Buy (Long)** — You're betting the price will go **up**. You profit when the price rises above your entry, and you lose if it drops below.
- **Sell (Short)** — You're betting the price will go **down**. You profit when the price falls below your entry, and you lose if it rises above.

> [!FOREX]
> "Going long" and "going short" are just trader-speak for "I think the price will go up" and "I think the price will go down." In forex, you can profit from prices going in either direction — you don't have to wait for things to go up.

## Order Ticket Fields

### Entry Price

- For a **market order**, this is filled in automatically with the current price.
- For a **limit order**, you type the price where you want the trade to start.

### Stop Loss (SL)

The Stop Loss is your safety net. It's a price level where the trade automatically closes to **limit your loss** if the market goes against you.

> [!WARNING]
> Always set a Stop Loss! Trading without one means a single bad trade could lose a huge chunk of your account. Think of it like a seatbelt — you hope you never need it, but you should always wear one.

### Take Profit (TP)

The Take Profit is the price level where the trade automatically closes to **lock in your profit**. When the market reaches this level, you win and the trade is done.

### Position Size (Units)

This controls how much currency you're trading. A bigger position means bigger potential profits, but also bigger potential losses.

Think of it like a bet — if you bet $1 on a coin flip, you can win or lose $1. If you bet $100, you can win or lose $100. Position size works the same way.

> [!TIP]
> If you're just starting out, use the smallest position size available. You can always increase it later once you're more comfortable. There's no rush.

## Drag-to-Set on Charts

Here's a cool feature: instead of typing numbers into the Order Ticket, you can **drag lines directly on the chart** to set your entry price, Stop Loss, and Take Profit.

Just grab the line and drag it up or down to the level you want. The Order Ticket updates automatically as you drag. This gives you a visual way to set your levels, which many traders find easier and more intuitive.

## Placing the Order

Once you've filled in all the fields:

1. Double-check your direction (Buy or Sell)
2. Confirm your Stop Loss and Take Profit are set
3. Click the **Place Order** button

For market orders, the trade starts immediately. For limit orders, it goes to the "Waiting" tab on the [Positions page](./01-positions.md) until the price reaches your entry level.

> [!NOTE]
> You can always cancel a pending (limit) order before it fills. Once a market order executes, though, it's live — you'll need to close it from the Positions page if you change your mind.
