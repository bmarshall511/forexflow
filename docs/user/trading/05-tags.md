# Tags

Tags are labels you attach to trades to help you organize and categorize them. Think of them like hashtags on social media — they don't change how the trade works, but they make it much easier to find, group, and analyze your trades later.

## What Are Tags?

A tag is just a short text label. You create whatever tags make sense for your trading. Some examples:

- `swing-trade` — A trade you planned to hold for several days
- `scalp` — A very quick trade held for just minutes
- `news-event` — A trade placed around an economic news release
- `practice` — A trade you placed just to learn, not for serious profit
- `high-confidence` — A trade where you felt very sure about your analysis
- `breakout` — A trade based on price breaking through a key level
- `revenge-trade` — A trade you placed emotionally after a loss (tagging these honestly helps you spot bad habits!)

> [!TIP]
> There's no "right" set of tags. Create ones that are meaningful to _you_ and your trading style. Even just a few simple tags can make a huge difference when reviewing your trades later.

## Creating Custom Tags

You can create a new tag at any time. There's no limit to how many tags you can have. To create a tag:

1. Open a trade's detail view or select trades from the Positions page
2. Look for the tags section
3. Type a new tag name and press Enter

The tag is created instantly and applied to the trade. You can reuse existing tags by selecting them from the dropdown that appears as you type — FXFlow remembers all your previous tags.

> [!NOTE]
> Tag names should be short and descriptive. Use hyphens instead of spaces (like `swing-trade` instead of `swing trade`). Keep them lowercase for consistency, though FXFlow isn't strict about this.

## Applying Tags

### To a Single Trade

Open any trade (from the Waiting, Live, or Closed tabs) and look for the tags area. Click to add one or more tags. You can apply as many tags as you want to a single trade.

### To Multiple Trades at Once

When reviewing your closed trades, you might want to tag several at the same time. Select multiple trades using the checkboxes, then apply a tag to all of them in one action. This is a big time-saver when you're catching up on tagging after a busy trading day.

## Filtering Trades by Tags

This is where tags really shine. On the Positions page, you can filter the trade list to show only trades with a specific tag.

For example:

- Filter by `swing-trade` to see only your longer-term trades
- Filter by `news-event` to see how you perform around economic releases
- Filter by `practice` to separate learning trades from serious ones

You can combine tag filters with other filters (like date range or currency pair) to drill down even further.

> [!TIP]
> Use tags together with the Analytics page for powerful insights. For example, you could find out "What's my win rate on swing trades?" or "Am I profitable on news events?" Tags make these questions answerable.

## Use Cases and Ideas

Here are some popular ways traders use tags:

| Tag              | Purpose                                          |
| ---------------- | ------------------------------------------------ |
| `trend-follow`   | Trades that follow the overall market direction  |
| `counter-trend`  | Trades going against the current trend           |
| `asian-session`  | Trades placed during the Asian market hours      |
| `london-session` | Trades placed during the London market hours     |
| `us-session`     | Trades placed during the US market hours         |
| `earnings`       | Trades around major economic reports             |
| `setup-a`        | A specific trading strategy you're testing       |
| `setup-b`        | Another strategy, so you can compare performance |
| `emotional`      | Trades you placed based on emotion, not logic    |

> [!WARNING]
> Be honest with your tags. It's tempting to only tag your winning trades as "high-confidence" after the fact. But tags are most useful when they're applied _before_ you know the outcome. Tag trades when you place them, not after they close.

## Why Tags Matter

Without tags, all your trades look the same in your history — just a list of currency pairs, dates, and profit/loss numbers. With tags, your trade history becomes a searchable, filterable database that helps you answer important questions about your trading.

Over time, patterns emerge. You might discover you're great at swing trades but terrible at scalping. Or that your news-event trades consistently lose money. These insights are gold for improving as a trader, and tags are what make them possible.
