# Charts

Charts are the visual heartbeat of trading. They show you how a currency's price has moved over time, drawn as a graph. In FXFlow, charts do even more — they show your active trades right on the chart so you can see exactly where your entry, stop loss, and take profit levels sit relative to the current price.

## Understanding Candlestick Charts

FXFlow uses **candlestick charts**, which are the most popular way traders view prices. Each "candle" represents one time period (like 1 hour, 4 hours, or 1 day depending on your timeframe setting).

Here's what a single candle tells you:

- **Green candle** — The price went _up_ during that time period. It opened low and closed higher.
- **Red candle** — The price went _down_ during that time period. It opened high and closed lower.
- **The thick part** (called the "body") — Shows where the price opened and closed.
- **The thin lines** (called "wicks" or "shadows") — Show the highest and lowest price reached during that period.

> [!FOREX]
> Imagine you're tracking the temperature throughout a day. A candle would show you: the temperature when you woke up (open), the temperature when you went to bed (close), the hottest point of the day (high wick), and the coldest point (low wick). Candlestick charts do the same thing, but with currency prices.

## Multi-Panel Layouts

You might want to watch several currency pairs at the same time. FXFlow lets you split your screen into multiple chart panels:

- **1 chart** — A single, large chart filling the whole area
- **2 charts** — Two charts side by side
- **4 charts** — A grid of four charts (2 rows of 2)

Each panel is independent — you can set a different currency pair and timeframe on each one. This is great for watching related markets at the same time.

### Selecting Instruments

Each chart panel has a dropdown at the top where you pick which currency pair to display. Type a pair name (like "EUR/USD") or scroll through the list. The chart updates instantly when you make a selection.

## Trade Overlays

This is where charts get really powerful. When you have an active trade on a currency pair, FXFlow draws lines directly on that chart:

- **Entry line** — A horizontal line showing where you entered the trade
- **Stop Loss (SL) line** — A red line showing your safety exit level (where the trade closes to limit your loss)
- **Take Profit (TP) line** — A green line showing your profit target (where the trade closes to lock in your gain)

These lines make it easy to see at a glance how your trade is doing. If the current price is between your entry and your TP, you're in profit. If it's between your entry and your SL, you're at a loss.

> [!TIP]
> Assign your open trades to chart panels to see the overlays. It turns an abstract number into something visual and much easier to understand.

### Assigning Trades to Panels

To see trade overlays, you need to assign a trade to a chart panel. You can do this from the chart panel's settings or from the Positions page. Once assigned, the entry, SL, and TP lines appear on the chart automatically.

## Real-Time Price Updates

Charts update in real time. As new price data comes in, the current (rightmost) candle grows and changes. You'll see prices ticking up and down live — this is the market in action.

The price axis on the right side of the chart always shows the latest price, so you always know where things stand right now.

## Mobile Experience

On a phone, there isn't enough space to show multiple charts side by side. Instead, FXFlow lets you **swipe between panels**. Each swipe moves you to the next chart, like flipping through pages.

> [!NOTE]
> Charts can feel overwhelming at first with all the candles and lines. That's completely normal! Start with a single chart on a currency pair you're interested in and just watch how it moves for a while. You'll start recognizing patterns quickly.
