# Your First Trade

Time to place your first trade. Don't worry — if you're using a practice account (and you should be), this is all with fake money. Nothing to lose, everything to learn.

## Step 1: Go to the Charts Page

Click on **Charts** in the sidebar. You'll see a live price chart — a graph that shows how the price of a currency pair has moved over time.

## Step 2: Pick a Currency Pair

At the top of the charts page, you can select which **currency pair** you want to look at.

> [!FOREX]
> A **currency pair** is two currencies shown together, like **EUR/USD**. The first currency (EUR = euros) is the one you're buying or selling. The second currency (USD = US dollars) is what you're paying with. The **price** tells you how much of the second currency you need to buy one unit of the first. For example, if EUR/USD = 1.0850, that means 1 euro costs 1.0850 US dollars.

For your first trade, pick **EUR/USD**. It's the most popular currency pair in the world and a great starting point.

## Step 3: Open the Order Ticket

Click the button to open the **Order Ticket** (the trading form). This is where you set up your trade.

## Step 4: Choose Your Order Type

You'll see two main options:

### Market Order

A market order means **buy or sell right now at the current price**. It's instant. Whatever the price is at this very moment, that's the price you get.

### Limit Order

A limit order means **buy or sell when the price reaches a level I choose**. You set a target price, and the trade only happens if the market reaches that price. Think of it like setting an alarm — "wake me up when the price hits this number."

> [!TIP]
> For your first trade, use a **Market Order**. It's simpler and you'll see results immediately.

## Step 5: Pick Your Direction

This is the big decision: do you think the price is going **up** or **down**?

> [!FOREX]
> **Buy (Long)** means you think the price will go **UP**. You're buying the first currency, hoping to sell it later for more. If EUR/USD is at 1.0850 and you buy, you make money if it goes to 1.0900 and lose money if it drops to 1.0800.

> [!FOREX]
> **Sell (Short)** means you think the price will go **DOWN**. You're selling the first currency, hoping to buy it back cheaper later. If EUR/USD is at 1.0850 and you sell, you make money if it drops to 1.0800 and lose money if it rises to 1.0900.

Not sure which to pick? That's totally fine for practice. Just pick one — the point is to learn the process.

## Step 6: Set Your Safety Nets

These two settings protect your money. **Always use them.**

> [!FOREX]
> A **Stop Loss** is an automatic exit that closes your trade if you're losing too much. You set a price level, and if the market hits that level, FXFlow tells OANDA to close the trade immediately. It's like a safety net that catches you before you fall too far. For example, if you buy EUR/USD at 1.0850, you might set a stop loss at 1.0820 — meaning you're okay losing up to 30 pips, but no more.

> [!FOREX]
> A **Take Profit** is the opposite — it automatically closes your trade when you've made enough money. You set a target price, and when the market reaches it, OANDA locks in your profit. If you buy at 1.0850, you might set a take profit at 1.0900, locking in 50 pips of profit.

> [!WARNING]
> Never trade without a stop loss. Prices can move fast, and without one, a small loss can become a very large loss in minutes. Always know your exit before you enter.

## Step 7: Set Your Position Size

> [!FOREX]
> **Position size** (also called lot size or units) is how much currency you're buying or selling. A bigger position means bigger potential profits AND bigger potential losses. Think of it like a volume knob — turning it up makes everything louder, both the good and the bad.

For your first practice trade, start small. Use the minimum or a small number of units. You can always go bigger later once you understand how it all works.

## Step 8: Place the Trade

Double-check everything looks right, then click the **Place Order** button.

Congratulations — you just placed your first trade!

## Step 9: Watch Your Trade Live

Go to **Positions** and click on the **Live** tab. You'll see your trade listed there with real-time information:

- **Currency pair** — What you're trading
- **Direction** — Buy or Sell
- **Entry price** — The price when you opened the trade
- **Current price** — Where the price is right now
- **Profit/Loss** — How much you're up or down, updating in real time

> [!FOREX]
> A **pip** is the smallest standard price movement in forex. For EUR/USD, one pip is 0.0001. So if the price moves from 1.0850 to 1.0851, that's a 1-pip move. Pips are how traders measure price changes — instead of saying "the price went up by 0.0023 dollars," they say "it went up 23 pips."

> [!FOREX]
> The **spread** is the tiny difference between the buying price and the selling price. When you first open a trade, you'll usually see a small negative number in your profit/loss column — that's the spread. It's essentially the broker's fee for executing your trade. It's normal and expected.

## Step 10: Close Your Trade

When you're ready to close the trade (either to lock in profit or cut a loss):

1. Find your trade in the **Positions > Live** tab
2. Click on the trade to see its details
3. Click the **Close** button
4. Confirm the closure

Your trade is now closed. The profit or loss is final.

## Step 11: Review in Trade History

Go to **Positions** and click the **Closed** tab. Your completed trade will appear here with a full summary:

- How long the trade was open
- Your entry and exit prices
- Your total profit or loss
- Whether the trade was closed manually, by stop loss, or by take profit

> [!TIP]
> Reviewing your closed trades is one of the best ways to learn. Look at what happened — did the price keep going after you closed? Did your stop loss save you from a bigger loss? Over time, this review process helps you make better decisions.
