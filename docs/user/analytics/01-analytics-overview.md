# Analytics Overview

The Analytics page is like a report card for your trading. It shows you what you are doing well, what you are doing badly, and where you can improve. There are six tabs, each showing a different angle on your performance.

## Overview Tab

The main feature here is the **equity curve** — a line chart that tracks your account balance over time.

> [!FOREX]
> **Equity curve**: Think of it like a graph of your bank account balance, plotted day by day. When the line goes up, you made money. When it goes down, you lost money. A smooth, upward-sloping line is every trader's dream.

You will also see headline stats like total profit/loss, number of trades, and your **win rate**.

> [!FOREX]
> **Win rate**: The percentage of your trades that made money. If you took 10 trades and 6 were profitable, your win rate is 60%. A high win rate sounds great, but it is not the whole story — you also need to look at _how much_ you win versus _how much_ you lose.

> [!FOREX]
> **Drawdown**: The biggest drop from a peak to a low point in your account. If your account hit $10,000 and then fell to $9,200, your drawdown is $800 (or 8%). Smaller drawdowns mean smoother sailing.

## Currency Pairs Tab

This tab breaks down your results by each currency pair you have traded (like EUR/USD or GBP/JPY). You can instantly see:

- **Win rate per pair** — which pairs you tend to get right
- **Total profit/loss per pair** — which pairs actually make you money
- **Number of trades per pair** — how often you trade each one

> [!TIP]
> If you notice one pair consistently loses money, consider removing it from your watchlist. Sticking to pairs you understand well is one of the simplest ways to improve.

## Sessions Tab

Forex trades around the clock, but the market is not equally active at all times. The Sessions tab groups your trades into four major trading sessions:

| Session  | Hours (UTC)  | Character                       |
| -------- | ------------ | ------------------------------- |
| Sydney   | 10 PM – 7 AM | Quietest, smaller price moves   |
| Tokyo    | 12 AM – 9 AM | Moderate activity, Asia-focused |
| London   | 7 AM – 4 PM  | Busiest, biggest moves          |
| New York | 12 PM – 9 PM | High activity, overlaps London  |

> [!TIP]
> You might discover you trade much better during London hours than late at night. Use this data to focus on your best hours and skip the ones where you consistently lose.

## Time of Day Tab

This is a **heatmap** — a grid where each square represents a specific day of the week and hour of the day. The squares are colour-coded:

- **Green** = profitable during that time slot
- **Red** = losses during that time slot
- **Grey/blank** = no trades taken

It is like a calendar that instantly shows you _when_ you trade well and _when_ you do not.

## Sources Tab

Every trade in FXFlow is tagged with _how_ it was placed:

- **Manual** — you placed it yourself through FXFlow
- **TradingView Alert** — an automated alert from TradingView triggered it
- **AI Trade** — the AI Trader system found and placed it
- **Trade Finder** — the scanner spotted a setup and auto-traded it

This tab shows profit/loss for each source, so you can see whether your manual trades beat the automated ones (or vice versa).

## Trade Quality Tab

This tab is for more advanced analysis. It includes three key metrics:

> [!FOREX]
> **R-multiple**: Measures how much you won (or lost) compared to how much you risked. If you risked $100 and made $300, that is a 3R trade. If you risked $100 and lost $50, that is -0.5R. Good traders aim for an _average_ R-multiple above 1.0.

> [!FOREX]
> **Profit factor**: Total money won divided by total money lost. A profit factor of 2.0 means you earned twice as much as you lost. Anything above 1.0 means you are profitable overall. Below 1.0 means you are losing money.

- **Payoff ratio**: The average winning trade divided by the average losing trade. If your average win is $200 and your average loss is $100, your payoff ratio is 2:1.

> [!NOTE]
> You do not need a high win rate to be profitable. A trader who wins only 40% of the time but has a payoff ratio of 3:1 will still make money in the long run. Focus on the _combination_ of win rate and payoff ratio.

## What To Do With All This Data

1. **Check weekly** — look at your equity curve and overall stats
2. **Spot patterns** — are certain pairs, sessions, or sources dragging you down?
3. **Make one change at a time** — adjust your trading based on what the data tells you, then check again after 20–30 trades
