**What surprised**

The test harness paid for itself on the _first run_. It surfaced three real hook bugs: the `pre-edit-no-any` regex had a lookbehind that rejected the most common `paramName: any` case; `pre-commit-requirements-sync`'s `-m` regex didn't consume whitespace before the quote; `structure-stale-refs` stripped `.md` extensions in cleanup. All three were hook-author errors that passed every mental model I had until deterministic tests hit them. Captured as LRN-0001, LRN-0002, LRN-0003 and fixed same-session.

Even more instructive: I fabricated the `v3`→`main` cutover timing twice in the same session. The first fabrication was a guess ("when Phase 1 completes"). The maintainer caught it. My "fix" compounded the error — I cited my own earlier speculative roadmap as authoritative and rewrote it to "Phase 14 cutover," which is still false because no phase-number-to-timing commitment exists. The maintainer caught that too. LRN-0005 captures both fabrications and the meta-pattern that makes the second worse than the first: **agent-authored plans are self-justifying feedback loops for future fabrications**. Guarded now by CLAUDE.md Non-negotiable #13 and rule 00 §2's new paragraph.

**What we learned about the `.claude/` config itself**

- The `_comment`-key pattern is a trap in JSON Schema-validated settings (LRN-0004, FM-0002). Documentation goes in neighboring README files, not inside strict-schema objects.
- Fail-open bootstrapping (ADR 0002) works as designed: every hook that depended on not-yet-shipped infrastructure stayed quiet until the infrastructure landed, then activated automatically. Confirmed by the test harness exercising both states.
- The post-edit-meta-log hook quietly wrote ~200 entries to `.claude/CHANGELOG.md` `[Unreleased]` across the day. When sub-phase completions promote `[Unreleased]` to versioned releases, the entries form a natural diff of what the agent touched and when.
- Generated-and-committed `.cursor/` artifacts (Sub-phase 7) made Cursor-parity enforcement cheap — one-liner `node scripts/sync-ide-rules.mjs` regenerates; the pre-commit hook catches anyone who forgets.
